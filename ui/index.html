Project Description

We simulate a JWST-like spacecraft operating near the Sun-Earth L2 Lagrange point using the Circular Restricted Three-Body Problem. Our project builds an end-to-end pipeline: starting from an Earth parking orbit, we design a transfer to the L2 region and then propagate a three-dimensional halo orbit in the rotating frame. We validate the dynamics using the Jacobi constant and long-term bounded motion, and we visualize the results with 3D animations in both inertial and rotating frames. The final interface lets viewers quickly grasp how JWST can reach L2 and maintain a stable halo orbit.


Conceptual Model: Sun-Earth CR3BP

We model JWST as a massless spacecraft moving under the gravity of the Sun and Earth using the Sun-Earth Circular Restricted Three-Body Problem (CR3BP). The two primaries move on circular orbits about their common center of mass, and the spacecraft is described in a rotating barycentric frame where the Sun and Earth are fixed on the \(x\)-axis. We follow the standard non-dimensional CR3BP equations given in Weber (2024) .

In this frame the state is \(\mathbf{X} = [x, y, z, \dot{x}, \dot{y}, \dot{z}]^\top\), and the CR3BP dynamics are

\[ \ddot{x} - 2\dot{y} = x - \frac{1-\mu}{\sigma^3}(x+\mu) - \frac{\mu}{\psi^3}(x-1+\mu), \] \[ \ddot{y} + 2\dot{x} = y - \frac{1-\mu}{\sigma^3}y - \frac{\mu}{\psi^3}y, \] \[ \ddot{z} = - \frac{1-\mu}{\sigma^3}z - \frac{\mu}{\psi^3}z, \] where \(\sigma = \sqrt{(x+\mu)^2 + y^2 + z^2}\) and \(\psi = \sqrt{(x-1+\mu)^2 + y^2 + z^2}\).

We integrate this 3D system to simulate two behaviors:

  • a transfer trajectory from a near-Earth orbit to the vicinity of \(L_2\)
  • a bounded halo orbit around \(L_2\) in the rotating frame.
The final simulation combines both parts, showing the full Earth-to-halo trajectory.

Validation: Face, Behavior & Analytical-Model Checks


Face validation

As shown previously in the 3D Animation Gallery, we visually inspect the simulated LEO-to-halo trajectory in the rotating frame. The 3D curve forms the expected tilted, eccentric loop around L2, with shape and amplitude matching published Sun–Earth halo-orbit geometry, providing a qualitative face validation that the motion looks physically reasonable.


Analytical-model validation

We compute the Jacobi constant along the trajectory and track C(t) - C(0) over a two-year window. The upper-right plot Jacobi Constant Drift shows that the deviation remains at the level of numerical roundoff, indicating that our CR3BP equations, non-dimensional scaling, and integrator are implemented correctly.


Behavior validation

We also monitor the distance from the spacecraft to L2 over the entire scenario. The lower-right plot LEO + Transfer + Halo Orbit Boundedness Check shows a nearly constant radius in LEO, a sharp change during transfer, and then a bounded, periodic radius in the halo phase, confirming successful arrival at the L2 region and stable long-term motion around it.
